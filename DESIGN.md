## Overview

bravesoft is a Tokyo-based studio that builds smartphone apps, websites, and event-tech products under the slogan **「あしたをオモシロク」(Make tomorrow more interesting)**. The site is unapologetically a *corporate marketing canvas* — bold, kinetic, photographic — engineered to broadcast scale (1,800+ projects, 3.5M+ event participants) without feeling stiff.

The aesthetic is **Energetic Modern Corporate** with a J-pop / event-tech twist. It pairs:

- A *hero-billboard* posture: oversized Japanese display type, vivid blue gradient slabs, photographic case-study cards.
- A *kinetic layer*: small UI elements (badges, ribbons, decorative type) tilt between -10° and +15°, slide on scroll, and lift on hover. Nothing in the page feels static.
- A *Swiss-meets-Tokyo* baseline: Noto Sans JP set heavy, generous whitespace, near-black ink on broadsheet-white surfaces.

The emotional tone is **confident, optimistic, slightly playful**. The design must read as a B2B partner you can trust with enterprise contracts, while still telegraphing the energy of a creative studio shipping consumer apps and live event experiences.

### Page archetypes

The system supports five recurring page templates. Each is composed from the same token set:

- **Marketing home** — kinetic, full-bleed, photographic. Largest type, most tilted decorative elements, service and case hero cards.
- **Index pages** (works, news, company, about us) — a centered bilingual page title over a segmented pill-tab row, followed by a card grid. Calm, white-dominant, no hero billboard.
- **Detail pages** (work case, news article) — a breadcrumb bar pinned under the header, a centered article container (720–880px), inline `link-on-light` links, mono date eyebrows, and hairline `grey-150` rules between sections.
- **Recruit / hiring** — its own sub-palette that adds deeper royal and navy blues (`#004190`, `#034391`), warm orange accents (`#DB8048`), and success greens (`#7AD1AC`). Uses a denser, more editorial layout with photography and quote cards. Treat these colors as *recruit-only accents* — do not use in the corporate marketing site.
- **Form / contact** — grounded on `surface-faint` fields, 3px-radius inputs, `outline-hairline` field borders, a single primary CTA.

## Colors

The palette is a **two-color system** elevated by neutrals: an electric brand blue and near-black ink, separated by a quiet warm-white field.

- **Brand Blue (`#0066FF`)** is the unmistakable identity color — used for primary CTAs, key links, hero backgrounds, the floating "scroll-up" pip, the underline on active nav, and the tinted icon circles that sit on hover-revealed work cards. Two practical button shades (`#2966FF` resting, `#2247FF` hover) feel slightly heavier on screen than the pure spec value and are preferred for solid pill CTAs.
- **Brand Blue Soft (`#E5F0FF` / hover `#C3DEFF`)** is the "tonal" companion — the background for the secondary "Contact us" pill that sits next to the primary CTA. It keeps two adjacent pills from competing.
- **Sky Blue (`#3F9CFF`)** and **Deep Blue (`#3366FF`)** appear in inline content (table-of-contents, list bullets, chart accents). Treat them as supporting accents, not buttons.
- **Brand Navy (`#253A58`)** is reserved for stat tiles and dark photographic overlays where pure black would feel too funereal. Often shipped at 80% opacity over imagery.
- **Ink (`#090A0A`)** is used for body text and the footer field. **Ink Strong (`#161717`)** is the slightly warmer near-black used for solid dark pills and chip backgrounds. **Ink Muted (`#333333` / `#333333CC`)** is for secondary captions and metadata.
- **Surface (`#FFFFFF`)** is the dominant page color. **Surface Subtle (`#F7F9FA`)** banded across alternating sections breaks the page into rhythmic horizontal zones without resorting to dividers. **Surface Chip (`#F2F4F5`)** is one tick darker, used for inactive tabs and chips.
- **Outlines** are *almost always* alpha black: `rgba(0,0,0,0.10)` for hairline card borders, `rgba(0,0,0,0.05)` for the ghostly internal dividers between news cards. On dark backgrounds, the inverse `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)` is used. The token `outline` resolves to `#090A0A` and is intended to be applied at **10% alpha**; `outline-variant` (`#000000`) at **5%**; and `outline-on-dark` (`#FFFFFF`) at **10%**. A solid `outline-hairline` (`#E3E5E5`) is used for the thin vertical bars between footer SNS icons and the date separators inside news cards.
- **Alpha conventions.** Several tokens are intended to be rendered with a fixed opacity rather than at full strength: `surface-overlay-light` (`#FFFFFF`) at **90%** for the floating navbar field; `surface-overlay-dark` (`#000000`) at **70%** for image-overlay caption blocks; `scrim` (`#000000`) at **50%** for modal backdrops; `ink-muted` (`#333333`) at **80%** for low-emphasis metadata. These compose with the page imagery to produce the brand's frosted, layered feel.

### The grey ramp

Interior pages (works, news, company) expose a proper neutral ramp that the marketing homepage barely uses. Memorize this mapping — it is the single biggest source of consistency in body text across the site:

- **`grey-700` / `#303437`** — solid dark grey for menu items, secondary body text, footer link text.
- **`grey-600` / `#404446`** — SNS icon captions, inline metadata.
- **`grey-500` / `#575757`** — long-form body text on the recruit template (used as an alternative to `ink-muted`).
- **`grey-400` / `#6C7072`** — primary muted / subtitle color. Used for page-subtitle English labels ("Our Works", "About Us"), card subtitles, and descriptions.
- **`grey-350` / `#72777A`** — footer meta, copyright, and legal captions.
- **`grey-300` / `#979C9E`** — breadcrumb links, very-low-emphasis timestamps and labels.
- **`grey-200` / `#CDCFD0`** — date separator slashes in news cards, disabled chips.
- **`grey-150` / `#E3E5E5`** — solid hairline rules and vertical dividers where alpha won't reliably render (e.g. thin 1px rules inside cards).
- **`grey-100` / `#EFEFEF`** — used on recruit template as a low-emphasis form field fill.

### Blue-link and deep-blue sub-palette

Long-form pages (news articles, privacy, recruit, company) use a slightly wider blue register than the homepage. These colors are reserved for *text links* and *editorial headings*:

- **`brand-blue-link` / `#0665FF`** — body text link color (slightly deeper than pure brand blue for better readability on white).
- **`brand-blue-link-visited` / `#1360BF`** — visited state for inline links.
- **`brand-blue-deep` / `#3366FF`** — TOC level-1 bullets and H2 accents.
- **`brand-blue-sky` / `#3F9CFF`** — TOC level-2 bullets and H3 accents; also the color of the hover-revealed "+" icon on service cards.
- **`brand-blue-hover-cyan` / `#00BBFF`** — the *only* hover text color; swaps in for `ink-strong` on group-hover in service tiles.
- **`brand-blue-royal` / `#004190`** and **`brand-blue-navy` / `#034391`** — recruit-only CTA fills; the hiring pages trade the bright homepage blue for something more "head-office formal."
- **`brand-blue-dusty` / `#5E7EAF`** — recruit meta/caption color.

The brand actively avoids tertiary chromatic accents in its corporate marketing. Energy is created through **scale, motion, and photography**, not a wide palette. The two exceptions both live on the **recruit template only**: `accent-orange` (`#DB8048`) for "Apply now" CTAs and `accent-green` (`#7AD1AC`) for success/available chips.

## Typography

Type is the loudest component of the system. Three families are in active use:

- **Noto Sans JP** is the workhorse for everything Japanese and Latin alike. It is set in a wide weight range from 400 (body) to 900 (display). Use **700 by default for headings**, **400 for paragraphs**.
- **Roboto Mono** is the precision register — used for English eyebrows above section titles ("OUR WORKS", "SERVICES"), big numerals on stat tiles, and any data label that benefits from the typewriter geometry. Often tracked +0.06–0.08em uppercase.
- **Roboto (italic 700)** is reserved for the *English subtitle* that sits below large Japanese section labels (e.g. the slanted "Our Works" under 「実績紹介」). Treat italic Roboto as a *graphic flourish*, not a text style — never run a paragraph in it.

Hierarchy principles:

- **Bilingual stacks.** Section headers are pairs: a large Japanese title (`headline-lg`) anchored by an italic English subtitle (`english-display-italic`) directly below it, often slightly indented. The two are visually one composition.
- **Display swings.** Hero billboards use very large type (80px on desktop, sometimes a decorative `300px` watermark of an English word that bleeds off-canvas). Outside heroes, headings rarely exceed 40px.
- **Generous body leading.** Body copy runs at line-height 1.75–1.85 to accommodate Japanese vertical rhythm and dense kanji.
- **Bold means clickable.** Pills, nav items, and chips are all 700-weight at 12–16px, leading 1.0, with no letter-spacing tweaks. Body text never goes bold for emphasis — it switches color to brand blue or wraps in an icon-tagged label instead.

## Layout

The site uses a **fluid 12-column grid inside a 1200–1280px max content width**, with stacked, full-bleed colored sections separating zones.

- **Section rhythm.** Vertical padding is `48px` mobile / `96px` desktop. Adjacent sections alternate `surface` and `surface-subtle` to telegraph "this is a new chapter."
- **Container padding.** Horizontal padding is `16px` on phones, `24–32px` on desktop. Hero photographic cards bleed to the viewport edge and round only their inner corners.
- **Spacing scale.** Strict 4px base, with the practical scale `4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96 / 128px`. Card gaps default to `16px`; cross-section gaps default to `32px`.
- **Asymmetry.** Sections often run headers flush-left and CTAs flush-right, with the body content offset into an 8-of-12 column slot. Avoid centered single-column compositions outside hero billboards *and* index pages (which are deliberately centered).
- **Floating header.** The top navigation is a *floating pill* — `surface-overlay-light` (`rgba(255,255,255,0.90)`) with a 10–12px backdrop-blur, sitting above the hero with `16px` lateral margins and full-pill rounding.
- **Header stack heights.** Reserve `56px` of top margin on mobile, `64px` on tablet, `80px` on desktop for the floating header clearance. The breadcrumb bar on interior pages sits *below* that clearance with its own `40px` height.

### Responsive breakpoints (11-step)

The site defines an unusually granular breakpoint ladder — far more than a typical three-step scale. Each step is used to micro-tune a single piece of UI (container width, card height, carousel peek, or a specific text size). Treat `md` (**769px**) and `xl` (**1280px**) as the two *primary* cutoffs; everything else is a fine-tuning layer:

| Token | Min-width | Primary use |
|:------|:----------|:------------|
| `sm0` | 536px | Container maxes out at 536px; two-up small cards become possible |
| `sm` | 540px | `.sm:h-full` tweak; small container adjust |
| `sm1` | 541px | First grid threshold — cards switch from stack to `sm1:grid` |
| `md1` | 767px | Pre-tablet fine-tune for card heights (`md1:h-[400px]`) |
| `md0` | 768px | First "tablet" block-level switch |
| **`md`** | **769px** | **Primary tablet→desktop cutoff.** Layouts reflow to 2–3 columns, header grows to 64px, Swiper carousels re-enable gestures, hover states activate |
| `md2` | 853px | Auto-center wide cards inside their container |
| `lg` | 1024px | Second desktop tier — enables larger card heights, 3-column grids |
| `lg0` | 1025px | Fine tune for 2-column spans inside 3-col grids |
| `lg2` | 1116px | Tuning the stat tile row height specifically |
| **`xl`** | **1280px** | **Primary desktop cutoff.** Full-width hero billboards, 80px header, larger type ramp, hover-lift animations enabled, arrow-pip reveals |

**Container maxes** track each breakpoint (container gets `max-width: {breakpoint}` at that query), producing a fluid-but-stepped central column.

**Hover hardening.** Hover-dependent motion is always gated on `(hover: hover) and (pointer: fine)` **plus** `min-width: 769px`. This means touch devices, small tablets, and stylus displays never see the lift/rotate/arrow-reveal animations — they use static state only. When re-implementing, honor that gate.

### Mobile patterns

- **Stacking.** Below `md` (769px) grids collapse to a single column. Horizontal Swiper carousels replace multi-column rows for service tiles and work thumbnails.
- **SP menu.** The navbar flips to a hamburger that opens a full-height `surface-chip` (`#F2F4F5`) overlay. Menu rows are 56px tall with `outline-hairline` separators. Secondary items nest beneath an expander, rendered with the same chip style.
- **Truncation.** Long Japanese titles use `truncate`, `text-ellipsis`, and `line-clamp-2` — never wrap beyond two lines inside cards.
- **Touch targets.** All taps are ≥44×44 CSS px. The mobile primary CTA is exactly **48px tall × 164px wide**; desktop grows to **56px tall × 172–320px wide**.
- **Rotation removal.** On mobile, decorative rotation is *reduced* (not removed) — ±3° to ±5° on chips, never ±10°+, to avoid text clipping at small sizes.
- **Card height.** Work-link cards are `88px` mobile / `182px` desktop; news cards are `126px` mobile / `196px` desktop. These heights are load-bearing for the scroll-reveal animation timings — keep them.

## Elevation & Depth

Depth is **whisper-soft**. The system avoids heavy drop shadows in favor of:

- **Tonal layering.** Switching between `surface` and `surface-subtle` does most of the work of separating zones.
- **Hairline borders.** Cards are bounded by `1px solid rgba(0,0,0,0.10)` rather than shadows. On dark photographic cards, the same hairline at `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)` simulates a light edge.
- **Stacked micro-shadows.** When elevation is needed (hover lift, modals), it is built from four near-invisible layers (1–4% black) producing a barely-perceptible aura, never a hard offset shadow.
- **Backdrop blur.** The floating navbar, sticky CTAs, and image-overlay captions all use `backdrop-filter: blur(10–16px)` to feel like sheets of frosted glass over the underlying media.
- **Hover lift.** Cards move up `4–8px` and gain the `hover-lift` shadow over `150ms`. Photographic work cards additionally reveal a circular brand-blue arrow pip in the top-right.

The result is depth that comes from *light and translucency*, not gravity.

## Shapes

The shape language is **pill-and-rounded-rect**.

- **Pills are everywhere.** Buttons, chips, badges, the floating navbar, the icon container of the hover arrow — all use `rounded-full` (`9999px`) or `rounded-pill` (`99px`). Pills imply approachability and reinforce the "tap me" affordance for a Japanese mobile-first audience.
- **Cards are softly rounded.** Photographic work cards, service cards, and news tiles use `rounded-lg` (`12px`) or `rounded-xl` (`16px`). Smaller utility containers (chips, inputs) use `rounded-sm` (`4px`) to `rounded-md` (`8px`).
- **Sharp = decorative only.** Square corners are reserved for full-bleed backgrounds and the dark footer. Avoid square corners on any interactive element.
- **Iconography.** Icons are line-based, 1.5–2px stroke, with rounded caps and joins. Solid icons appear only inside circular brand-blue pips. Arrow icons are the most-repeated motif (CTA arrows, "more" arrows, scroll prompts).

## Components

### Buttons & CTAs

- **Primary CTA** is a brand-blue solid pill, 56px tall, 32px lateral padding, white 700-weight 14–16px label, often with a chevron icon. Hover deepens to `#2247FF`; never changes radius or scale.
- **Secondary CTA** is the same shape on a `brand-blue-soft` (`#E5F0FF`) field with brand-blue text. Used adjacent to primaries.
- **Dark utility pill** (`#161717` solid, white text, 40px tall) appears in the header for "お問い合わせ" / "Contact" — it is intentionally smaller and quieter than the in-content CTAs.
- **Ghost pill** (transparent, ink text) is used for navbar links and tertiary actions; the hover is opacity 0.7, never a background.
- **Circular icon pip** (`48px`, brand blue, white arrow) is used as a hover-only affordance on imagery. It animates from opacity 0 to 1 over `150ms`.

### Cards

- **Work card** is photographic-first: full-bleed image with rounded corners, hairline border, and a small chip overlay in the bottom-left naming the project. On hover the image scales `1.02x`, the chip slides up, and the brand-blue arrow pip fades in.
- **Service card** comes in two flavors: a *brand-blue hero* card (white headline, `300px` decorative watermark of the English service name bleeding off-canvas) and a *mono dark* card (`#161717`, white text, used for secondary services). Both are `rounded-xl`, `32px` padded.
- **News card** is white, `12px` rounded, hairline-bordered, with a mono date eyebrow above a 2-line title. Cards are joined horizontally by a 1px `rgba(0,0,0,0.10)` divider that doubles as a subtle hover indicator.
- **Stat tile** uses navy (`#253A58` / 80% over imagery) with a Roboto Mono numeral and a small Noto Sans JP unit label.

### Chips & Badges

- **Tag chip** is a soft-grey (`#F2F4F5`) pill with ink text, `4px 12px` padding, used inside cards for category labels.
- **Brand chip** swaps the surface for `#E5F0FF` and the text for brand blue — used for "NEW", "DX", and product-family labels.
- **Tilted callout** is a brand-blue chip rotated `-4°` to `+5°`, often with an emoji or icon, used as a marketing flourish next to imagery. Use sparingly — at most one per visible viewport.

### Navigation

- **Floating navbar** is a 90%-white pill with `blur(12px)` backdrop, hairline outline, and 12px lateral padding. Links are `label-md` ghost pills; the active link is brand-blue with a `2px` underline.
- **Mobile menu** opens as a full-screen overlay with `surface-chip` rows, 56px tall, divided by hairlines, and animates from the right at `300ms`.

### Sections & Hero

- **Hero billboard** is a full-bleed brand-blue or photographic field with a Japanese display headline, an English italic subtitle, a one-sentence body, and a primary CTA. A second decorative element (oversized rotated text, pip cluster, or tilted chip) sits on the opposite side.
- **Section header** is the bilingual title pair (`headline-lg` Japanese over `english-display-italic` English) flush-left, with a "もっと見る" (See more) ghost-pill flush-right.

### Footer

- Solid `#090A0A` field with white text. Three columns of link lists, a logo block, and small caption-grey legal text. Spacing is generous (`64px` vertical padding) — the footer is treated as the final restful exhale after the kinetic body.
- **Footer links** use `footer-link` (`grey-700` / `#303437`) — *not* pure white — which creates a soft, readable grey-on-black.
- **SNS icon row** sits above the link columns. On mobile each icon is a **36px circular pill** in `brand-blue-card-hover` (`#EEF2FA`); on desktop they sit on a `surface` white pill with a label beside them. Vertical `outline-hairline` (`#E3E5E5`) bars separate icons on desktop, hidden on mobile.
- **Legal row** at the bottom uses `footer-meta` (`grey-350` / `#72777A`) and separates link items with a 1px × 12px vertical hairline bar.

### Breadcrumb bar

Interior pages (works, news, company, privacy, recruit) add a fixed-position breadcrumb strip **immediately below the floating header**.

- Background: solid `surface` white, with a `border-b` at `rgba(9,10,10,0.10)`.
- Height: `40px`. Padding: `8px 16px` mobile, `8px 24px` desktop.
- Text: `body-sm`, `grey-300` for ancestor links, `ink-strong` for the current page. Separator is a small chevron in `grey-200`.
- Max-width: `1200px`, horizontally scrollable with `truncate` on overflow — breadcrumb never wraps.

### Segmented tabs & filter pills

The index pages (works, news) ship two tab patterns:

- **Segmented pill wrapper** (`tab-segmented-wrapper`) — a `surface-chip` pill 4px padded, containing 2–3 ghost-pill segments. The active segment fills with solid `surface` white and shifts its label to **brand blue**. Used for "All / Apps / Web" style binary splits. Width is a fixed 200px mobile / 248px desktop.
- **Filter pill row** (`tab-filter-pill`) — individual `surface-chip` pills at `rounded-md` (8px) with 12px horizontal padding. Active state fills with `ink-strong` and switches text to white. Used for news category filtering where the set is open-ended. Scrolls horizontally on mobile.

### Page header (interior pages)

- Centered `headline-lg` title in `ink-strong` (`#161717`), followed directly by a regular-weight **English subtitle** in `grey-400` (`#6C7072`) at `body-lg` / `body-md`. **This is a different subtitle treatment from the homepage** — the homepage uses italic Roboto 700; interior pages use regular Noto Sans JP for legibility.
- Optional small decoration (tilted `-4°` chip) flush to the subtitle.
- `mt-[56px]/md:mt-[64px]/xl:mt-[80px]` top margin to clear the floating header.
- `mb-14` / `mb-56px` bottom margin before the first content row.

### News card

- Horizontal layout on desktop (date stack | title | arrow), vertical stack on mobile.
- **Date stack** uses a three-piece mono composition: `number-mono-lg` (32px desktop, 24px mobile) for the day, a 45° rotated `/` divider in `grey-200`, the two-digit day in smaller mono. The year sits beneath in 12–16px mono `grey-200`, rotated `-90°` on mobile to read vertically as a tiny gutter element.
- **Category tag** uses `news-category-tag` — a minimal `surface-chip` chip with `ink-strong` text, 2×4px padding, no border.
- **Title** is `body-lg` at `line-clamp-2`. Hover deepens to `ink-strong` 700-weight.

### Long-form article / detail page

- Content column centered at **720–880px** max width.
- Body text: `body-lg` (16px × 1.85) in `ink` or `grey-500`.
- Inline links: `link-on-light` (`brand-blue-link` / `#0665FF`), underlined on hover.
- H2 accent: a 4px-wide `brand-blue` bar to the left of the heading.
- H3 accent: a `brand-blue-sky` circle bullet before the heading.
- Tables alternate `surface` and `surface-faint` rows with a `12px 16px` cell padding and an `outline-hairline` divider.
- Blockquote: left-aligned, indented 24px, with a 2px `brand-blue` left border; text color remains `grey-500`.

### Recruit template (isolated sub-system)

The hiring pages are *visually related* but use their own palette. When building a recruit page, treat it as a distinct skin:

- Primary CTA becomes `hero-button-royal` in `brand-blue-royal` (`#004190`) with navy hover.
- "Apply now" pills use `accent-orange-pill` (`#DB8048` on white text).
- "Open position" pills use `accent-green-pill` (`#7AD1AC`).
- Body text drops to `grey-500` (`#575757`).
- Content grid uses `surface-faint` (`#F7F7F7`) as the primary section background instead of `surface-subtle`.
- Do **not** import this palette back into the marketing or product pages.

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do**

- Pair every Japanese display title with an italic English subtitle directly below.
- Reach for the brand blue for *the single most important action in view* and let the rest stay neutral.
- Use motion: scroll-reveal slides, hover lifts, tilted micro-elements. The brand reads "boring corporate" without them.
- Let photography do the color work. The palette is intentionally narrow because hero imagery carries the chromatic load.
- Round everything interactive (`rounded-full` on actions, `rounded-lg`+ on containers).

**Accessibility caveats**

- The secondary brand-blue pill (`#0066FF` text on `#E5F0FF` field) clears WCAG AA only at **large text or bold ≥14pt** (it sits at ~4.2:1 normal text). Always render its label in 700-weight, 14px+, exactly as the production site does.
- The sky-blue (`#3F9CFF`), success (`#00A86B`), recruit orange (`#DB8048`), and recruit green (`#7AD1AC`) tones are not safe for white body text at small sizes. Reserve them for *large* pills (14px+ bold) and icons; never run paragraph text in white over these fills.
- The breadcrumb `grey-300` (`#979C9E`) sits at ~2.78:1 against white. This matches the production site, but it **fails WCAG AA for normal text**. Either bump to `grey-400` (`#6C7072`, 4.63:1) for accessibility-critical deployments, or keep at `grey-300` only for non-essential ancestor crumb links and ensure the current page is rendered in `ink-strong`.
- Body text and headlines must use **ink** (`#090A0A`) or **brand blue** (`#0066FF`). When color-coding status, pair color with an icon or label to preserve meaning for low-vision users.
- The rich grey ramp (`grey-700` through `grey-300`) is *designed* to produce a soft, broadsheet feel. Do not substitute pure black — the production site intentionally never uses `#000`.

**Don't**

- Don't introduce tertiary brand colors (greens, purples, oranges). Status colors only — and even those are rare.
- Don't pair brand blue with any blue outside the defined palette; the gap between `#0066FF` and `#3F9CFF` is intentional.
- Don't use thin (300/100) Noto Sans JP weights — Japanese characters at small sizes need 400 minimum.
- Don't centre full-page layouts. The grid is asymmetric, with text flush-left and CTAs flush-right by default.
- Don't use hard offset drop shadows. Depth is built from translucency and tonal shifts.
- Don't tilt or rotate body text, headlines, or interactive controls. Rotation is a *decorative* device only — chips, ribbons, watermark text.
